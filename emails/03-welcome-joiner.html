The commands that matter most

Egregore works through slash commands in your terminal. You don't need to learn them all — these four are the core loop:

/activity See what happened today. Who worked on what. Open threads and handoffs waiting for you.
/handoff End a session with a summary for the next person. This is how context flows between people.
/reflect Capture an insight worth keeping. Decisions, patterns, things you noticed.
/ask Ask a teammate something — async. They'll see it on their next session.
 
 

What to do in your first session

Open your terminal, navigate to the egregore directory, and run claude. The system will orient you — show you what's happening, who's working on what, and where you might plug in. Don't overthink it. Just start working on whatever matters to you. The context generation happens automatically.

The magic of Egregore is that you don't need to do anything special to contribute to the shared intelligence. You just work. The substrate captures the rest.
