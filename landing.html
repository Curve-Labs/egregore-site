Interface
Four commands. That's the whole product.
/activity
Everything your team is doing, in one view.
/handoff
Pass the baton. Knowledge captured, context preserved.
/invite
Send a link. They run one command. They're in.
/quest
Questions worth pursuing together.

More commands unlock as you use it — ammo we haven't fired yet.

Loop
Work. Handoff. Repeat.
  Open Claude Code
          │
  Egregore greets you
  shows what's happening
          │
  "What are you working on?"
          │
  You work — with full team context
          │
  /handoff when you're done
          │
  Next person picks up
  exactly where you left off
Substrate
Everything is a file.

Markdown files in a git repo. Readable by humans, indexed for Claude. No database. No hosted services. Just a repo your team shares.

my-team/
├── people/       who's on the team
├── handoffs/     session-to-session notes
├── knowledge/    decisions, findings, patterns
├── sessions/     what happened when
└── soul.md        who this team is
Why Open Source
It was too fun to keep to ourselves.

We built Egregore for ourselves — a small team spread across time zones, trying to build something ambitious without losing our minds.

Sessions stopped disappearing. Meetings became searchable artifacts. New people got context in minutes instead of days.

The team got smarter whether or not any one person was in the room.

What's Next
This is the core. There's more.

Internally, we run a version with a knowledge graph connecting every session, person, and decision. Meeting analysis that cross-references insights across months. Autonomous agents that synthesize while you sleep.

We're packaging that. It's coming.
